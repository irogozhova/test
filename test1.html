Oh Aurora Borealis!

The aurora is produced by solar wind particles guided by Earth's field lines to the top of the atmosphere.

Auroras are occasionally seen in temperate latitudes, when a magnetic storm temporarily enlarges the auroral oval.

Powerful X-class flares create radiation storms that produce auroras and can give airline passengers flying over the poles small radiation doses.

See them yourself

For the best chances to see the northern lights, or aurora borealis, head to Northern Norway or Svalbard between late autumn and early spring.
